This guide addresses a specific failure mode: startups that receive positive customer feedback but fail to achieve meaningful adoption or traction (usage or revenue).</span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c1">The framework below synthesizes insights from advising thousands of startups and borrows from neuroscience, behavioral economics, and human dynamics to explain why customers say they love your product but don&#39;t use it.</span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c11 c0">Goals for This Guide</span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c24"><span class="c1">Give you tools, frameworks and mental models to:</span></p><p class="c3"><span class="c1"></span></p><ul class="c9 lst-kix_7dnjzqimuq8d-0 start"><li class="c13 li-bullet-0"><span class="c1">Avoid company death.</span></li><li class="c13 li-bullet-0"><span class="c1">Get customers to enthusiastically pay you for your product.</span></li><li class="c13 li-bullet-0"><span class="c1">Have foundational metrics to raise VC (if that&rsquo;s your goal and you meet other VC criteria).</span></li></ul><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c11 c0">Failure Mode</span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c1">You&rsquo;ve spoken with customers and listened to their frustrations, and complaints. You&rsquo;ve shown them your product and they seem enthusiastic about it and said &ldquo;yes&rdquo; to the question: &ldquo;would you use this?&rdquo;. And yet, you hear crickets when it comes to actual adoption and traction. </span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c22">The problem isn&#39;t your product. The problem is physics and is rooted in the innovator&rsquo;s blindspot. </span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c11 c0">The Innovator&rsquo;s Blindspot</span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c1">The way you see the world is both an incredible advantage as well as the source of a blindspot: your customers are not like you. </span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c1">As a founder you embrace change, generate ideas, and see opportunity everywhere, most humans (i.e. your customers), however, are optimized for something entirely different: survival through conservation of energy. They want to make it through their last Zoom meeting, grab a glass of wine, and zone out to Netflix.</span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c1">This isn&#39;t laziness. This is biology. </span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c1">And physics helps to overcome the barrier. </span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c1">Here are the 5 laws of the Physics of Product Market Fit.</span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><h3 class="c10" id="h.yntc60u4p89l"><span class="c11 c0">Law #1: The Law of Inertia</span></h3><p class="c8"><span class="c26 c0"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c0 c28">Principle:</span><span class="c26 c22">&nbsp;A human being defaults to the status quo unless acted upon by a force.</span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c26 c22"></span></p><ul class="c9 lst-kix_ep4hnt4y9m8s-0 start"><li class="c4 li-bullet-0"><span class="c1">Human biology optimizes for energy conservation. This manifests as status quo bias which is the demonstrated preference for maintaining current conditions over adopting new ones, even when the new option is objectively superior.</span></li></ul><p class="c8 c23"><span class="c1"></span></p><ul class="c9 lst-kix_ep4hnt4y9m8s-0"><li class="c4 li-bullet-0"><span class="c22">Neuroscience confirms this isn&#39;t laziness but </span><span class="c18"><a class="c20" href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2018/09/180918090849.htm&amp;sa=D&amp;source=editors&amp;ust=1759449760328788&amp;usg=AOvVaw3P3ilaV-jqFqR0SUYm-Fnc">evolutionary optimization</a></span><span class="c1">. Our cognitive systems prioritize efficient energy use for survival-critical functions: safety assessment, resource acquisition, and social bonding.</span></li></ul><p class="c8"><span class="c11 c0"></span></p><p class="c10"><span class="c0">Implication:</span><span class="c1">&nbsp;Your primary competition is not other products. It is your customer&#39;s current workflow and their biological resistance to and fear of change.</span></p><p class="c8"><span class="c1"></span></p><ul class="c9 lst-kix_jcdymij8mel0-0 start"><li class="c4 li-bullet-0"><span class="c1">Change introduces uncertainty.
